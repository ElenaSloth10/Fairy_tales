Kaminchyk and Prominchyk

The old winding road, which on both sides had long been overgrown with ancient trees, led the two best friends. They were little otter cubs called Kaminchyk and Prominchyk. The friendly morning sun made its way through the thick leaves, illuminated the dew drops on the green leaves of the April grass like scattered diamonds. Dawn predicted the beginning of a new day, as if the beginning of a new life. It was so nice to look at the little animals, who were happy with the first rays of light and cheerfully had a friendly conversation.

The path led them to a beautiful city where Kaminchyk once visited. He often mentioned this small, town of ordinary animals, who daily engaged in ordinary affairs, building and improving houses and streets. The alleys meandered in an elegant ribbon, emphasizing the slenderness of the buildings. Long vines of ivy and grapes diluted this symmetry with bright spots. Instead, parks and squares were characterized by balanced forms and evenly trimmed plants. All this was often surrounded by the intoxicating aroma of morning coffee or freshly baked pies with cherries, apples, and currants. With these memories, it was as if they were wrapped in a warm shawl tied by their grandmother and given a cup of hot cocoa in their hands after a walk with bare feet in the morning dew.

However, approaching the city, the animals could not believe their eyes. The once bright and welcoming city was hard to recognize.

The sun's rays were blocked by heavy lead clouds. A thick fog enveloped the streets, like smoke from a raging fire. And it was like a huge soap bubble, which tightly covered all areas like a metal sarcophagus, that prevented him from dissipating. Now it was difficult to see well thought-out parks, developed avenues and green squares. The aroma of coffee shops and bakeries faded, the bright signs of small shops faded, the city fountain splashed joylessly. Stubborn Kaminchyk still wanted to see the once bright colors, but even there were no words to describe such a striking change.

Then the purposeful Prominchyk decided to find out what happened. He confidently headed down the winding sidewalk straight to the central square. There it was not difficult to find preoccupied animals, gloomily immersed in everyday affairs. They quickly approached the sullen badger who was selling newspapers.

- Good morning! - Prominchyk took the floor.

In response, the old badger nodded his head and continued to unfold whole stacks of black and white paper. Baby otter continued the conversation. This time Kaminchyk said:

- Good sir. I have been to your city and I remember it completely different. Won't you tell me what happened? What happened to its inhabitants?

The seller, not at all surprised by such a question, began his story with difficulty and tiredness:

- The city was like an ordinary city. And then one day a terrible wind arose, everything hummed and the animals were frightened. A huge and scary black bird flew right behind the roar. With his strong claws, he began to tear the roofs of houses. He broke windows and felled trees with his beak. We have never seen such burning anger in the eyes of a bird. And everything is so instantaneous. As if someone pierced the painting of a talented artist with an arrow and a big hole remained in the middle of the bright picture.

Baby otters were amazed by such a story. But after looking at each other, Kaminchyk belligerently asked:

- So where is he now? Must overcome immediately!

- It's too late to chase him. Very quickly, the feathered turned into black clots and fell to the ground. They firmly stuck to the backs of heads of the animals as sinewy octopuses, sucking all the sparks from their eyes. No one could do anything, we live with such a scourge.

The friends looked around and looked closely at the passers-by. And indeed, each of them wore an octopus on the back of their head, which grabbed tightly and clutched their heads. It was a pity for the residents and the once flourishing city. But what to do?

They thanked the seller and went to the nearest bench. It was necessary to think over such a frightening story. Baby otters sat and consulted, they really wanted to return the smiles, dispel the lingering fog and let the spring rays reach the streets of the city.

- We need to return the colors, - said Prominchyk.

- This is not enough, - answered Kaminchyk.

- So. Large-scale and decisive actions are needed here. We will create our own sorcery. Let's go, I have an idea!

Prominchyk resolutely got up and, full of his enthusiasm, headed across the entire square. Kaminchik immediately went after him. While wandering the streets for a short time, they came across a small stationery store. They bought all the colorful chalk in it and put it in their backpack.

The plan of action has already been adopted, all that remains is to implement the idea into life. They headed to the largest square of the ancient city. Got new purchases and started painting their birds. Passers-by looked around in surprise and incomprehensibility, did not understand the intentions of the travelers, but did not interfere with them.

Kaminchyk and Beaminchik mixed and alternated colors. They boldly experimented with shapes, sizes and colors. The birds came out sometimes big, sometimes small. They resembled already familiar species of birds - pigeons, titmice or storks, then they looked like fantastic fictional animals that only had wings and a beak. Some had a majestic appearance, others funny. Baby otters boldly fantasized and showed creativity. They painted as they could. And very soon, almost the entire asphalt was bursting with bright colors. Then the friends blew with persistent faith and lifted all the birds into the sky. It looked like a real miracle. The spirit was taken away from such an action.

Colorful wings flew along avenues and alleys. Magical creatures began to carefully and safely envelop the townspeople, and peck black octopuses with their strong beaks. Until they fell off and crumbled into ashes.

The little otters had to draw and cast spells for a long time. And the multi-colored wings work to return all the sparks to the eyes. Little by little, the smiles began to return, and the fog began to dissipate. The friends noticed that their idea was working and even more diligently drew beautiful lines with colorful chalk, forming living creatures.

The animals seemed to wake up. Warm rays of the sun passed through the remnants of the fog and reflected from the smiling eyes of the townspeople. As if the holiday has come. They began to rebuild houses and create new, even better and better ones. Later, the soap bubble burst.

At that point, baby otters looked around. They did a good job. It's good for the soul. It is already evening. For the first time in a long time stars could be seen. So bright, scattered by a careless hand, they only emphasized the greatness of the moon. Quiet and peaceful all around. But it is known for sure - tomorrow will be the next pleasant day and the sun will rise over the glorious city.

Now Kaminchyk was able to experience the same memories and give the same impressions to Prominchyk. This became the best reward.
